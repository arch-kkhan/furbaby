How might we improve the accessibility to quality dog interaction locally?

With an intuitive map, earned rewards, social engagement and dog-friendly suggestions, Furbabies will reduce your dog’s separation and social anxiety by promoting mental and physical well-being for both you and your furbaby.

Initial Concept

Originally, we researched the need for at home video monitoring for professional pet owners.

Final Concept: The process of pivoting

We found that pet owners weren’t interested in an at-home video monitoring system while they were away at work. Especially, they’re not worried when their pets were well trained or grown older. However, they were more concerned with the entertainment of their pet and their social anxiety when out in public.

Research Approach

Our team targeted audiences at the pet saturated places such as parks with heavy traffic, pet stores, and local dog parks. We initially posted a survey with about 12 questions, conducted about 10 street interviews and 4 user interviews. Everyone that participated was a pet owner. Our initial user research revealed that primary users wouldn’t be as interested in an at-home video monitoring system for their dog, but more so reliable entertainment and an increase in social interaction with their dogs. With these new findings, we pivoted our user research to focus more on entertainment and interaction within the dog community. With the data from the tailored survey and street interview questions, we transformed our objective to focus on the reduction of separation and social anxiety through the social engagement of the dog community.

Currently, there are mobile applications that connect dog owners to local dog services and products. They satisfy the proximity concept that users prefer while exploring for appropriate, however they really don’t have the social aspects that allows you to virtually engage with other dog owners and potentially earn rewards.

Team & My role

Our team consisted of Asante Bell, Cindy Guo and myself. As this was one of our first UX Projects, our desire was to understand and analyze each phase together. Throughout the three weeks of research and design, we collaborated extensively. There were discussions and a lot of debates which was a guiding force that helped us grow as designers and collaborators.

I like, I wish, What if?

After conducting our user research and interviews, we needed to creatively release our ideas. The “I like, I wish, What if?” exercise really helped eliminate our limited thoughts. As designers, we had to put ourselves in the shoes of our potential users.

“I like finding places with water available for my dog.”

“I like to create a profile for my dog.”

“I wish I could quickly find and compare dog-friendly places.”

“What if I can map my experience with my dog? What if our energies were recorded after every social interaction?”

“What if my dog had his own scavenger game planned for him…like a gaming, virtual/physical experience?”
